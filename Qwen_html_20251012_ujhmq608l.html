Kahraman Kostas

PhD in Computer Science | IoT Security • Machine Learning • Indoor Positioning

I hold a PhD in Computer Science from Heriot-Watt University, Edinburgh, where my research focused on IoT security—particularly device identification and attack detection using machine learning. I previously worked as a Researcher at Huawei’s Edinburgh R&D Centre, developing ML solutions for indoor positioning using geospatial data.

My MSc in Computer Networks and Security (Distinction) from the University of Essex centered on ML-based network intrusion detection. Before academia, I taught computer science in Turkey for seven years and contributed to national digital education initiatives like the Fatih Project.

Currently, I serve as a National Education Specialist at the Ministry of National Education, focusing on educational systems for overseas students. Outside work, I enjoy Turkish literature, classical poetry, hiking, and photography.
