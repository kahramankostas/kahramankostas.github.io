About Me

I hold a PhD in Computer Science from Heriot-Watt University, Edinburgh, where my research focused on IoT security, particularly device identification and attack detection. My broader interests include computer networks, indoor positioning, and practical applications of machine learning.

I previously worked as a Researcher at Huawei's Edinburgh R&D Centre, developing machine learning solutions for indoor positioning using geospatial data. I hold an MSc in Computer Networks and Security (Distinction) from the University of Essex, with my thesis centered on using machine learning for network attack detection.

I am currently a National Education Specialist at the Ministry of National Education, focusing on educational systems for overseas students. In addition to my professional work, I have a keen interest in Turkish literature, etymology, classical poetry, hiking, and photography.
